# Output Template

Use this template when the user wants a polished growth-stock report.

## Single-Name Template

### 1. Thesis

Write one short paragraph:

- what the company does
- why growth may persist
- what the stock needs to prove next

### 2. Why this could work

Use 3-5 bullets covering:

- structural demand
- product or cost advantage
- evidence from recent results
- why expectations may still be too low

### 3. Why this could fail

Use 3-5 bullets covering:

- deceleration risk
- margin or dilution risk
- competition
- financing or customer concentration

### 4. What to watch next

List the metrics that would change the view:

- revenue growth
- gross margin
- ARR or backlog
- capex
- share count
- customer wins or losses

### 5. Stock view

Conclude with:

- attractive now
- attractive on pullback
- watch only
- avoid for now

State what would upgrade or downgrade the rating.

## Two-Stock Comparison Template

Use a symmetrical table or mirrored bullets covering:

- business quality
- growth durability
- margin path
- balance-sheet flexibility
- dilution risk
- valuation support
- catalyst timing

Then finish with:

- better business
- better stock
- higher upside
- lower risk

Do not let one side get a much deeper treatment than the other.

## Backtest Addendum

If you include a backtest, add a short separate block:

- data window
- strategy or signal definition
- total return
- CAGR
- max drawdown
- benchmark comparison if used
- one-sentence interpretation

Keep the interpretation modest. A simple backtest is a decision aid, not proof.
