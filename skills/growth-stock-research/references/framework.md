# Framework

Use this reference when a stock question needs a deeper first-principles breakdown.

## 1. Business Engine

Explain the business as a compact system:

- Who pays?
- What pain point is being solved?
- Why now?
- What constrains supply?
- What allows the company to earn excess returns?

Preferred formula:

`value creation = demand growth x market share gains x unit economics x reinvestment efficiency`

If one term is weak, say so directly.

## 2. Durable Growth Test

Treat growth as durable only if several of these are true:

- The category itself is expanding
- The company has a distribution or product advantage
- Capacity can scale without destroying gross margin
- Customers face switching costs or integration friction
- Management can reinvest at attractive incremental returns

Red flags:

- Growth driven mainly by one-off customer ramps
- Revenue scale rising while gross margin falls structurally
- Heavy SBC masking weak operating discipline
- Equity or converts repeatedly funding the core business

## 3. Stock-Market Layer

A great business can still be a poor stock if:

- expectations are already extreme
- near-term numbers are likely to disappoint
- dilution is underappreciated
- the narrative is ahead of actual monetization

Ask:

- What is already in the price?
- Which metric matters most to marginal buyers right now?
- What future financing is the market assuming away?

## 4. Comparison Template

When comparing two growth stocks, score each area from 1-5:

- revenue durability
- margin quality
- balance-sheet strength
- capital intensity
- customer concentration risk
- valuation support
- catalyst clarity

Do not average blindly. Explain which factors matter most for this pair.

## 5. Research Output Template

Use this structure for fuller writeups:

1. Company snapshot
2. First-principles growth engine
3. Evidence supporting the bull case
4. Evidence supporting the bear case
5. What the next 2-4 quarters need to show
6. Why the stock may outperform or underperform from here
7. Confidence level and key unknowns
